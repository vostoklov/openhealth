# Hypothesis: Caffeine Cutoff at 2PM Improves Deep Sleep

## Metadata

- **Author:** community (example hypothesis)
- **Status:** proposed
- **Created:** 2026-04-05
- **Required connectors:** any sleep tracker (Oura, Apple Watch, Garmin, Whoop)
- **Required categories:** sleep, nutrition (optional — for caffeine tracking)

## Summary

Stopping caffeine intake after 2:00 PM will increase average deep sleep duration by at least 15 minutes within 14 days, compared to a 14-day baseline period with unrestricted caffeine.

## Background

Caffeine has a half-life of approximately 5-6 hours. A cup of coffee at 4 PM still has half its caffeine active at 10 PM. Many sleep researchers suggest a cutoff of 6-8 hours before bedtime. This hypothesis tests whether a 2 PM cutoff (approximately 8 hours before a typical 10 PM bedtime) produces measurable improvements in deep sleep specifically.

## Protocol

### Intervention

Stop consuming all caffeine (coffee, tea, energy drinks, chocolate) after 2:00 PM local time for the experiment duration. Morning caffeine is permitted.

### Duration

- **Baseline period:** 14 days (track sleep normally, no caffeine restrictions)
- **Experiment period:** 14 days (no caffeine after 2:00 PM)

### What to measure

1. **Deep sleep duration** (minutes) — from sleep tracker
2. **Total sleep duration** (minutes) — from sleep tracker
3. **Sleep efficiency** (%) — from sleep tracker
4. **HRV during sleep** (ms) — from sleep tracker (if available)
5. **Subjective energy level** (1-10) — manual input, morning after waking

### Success criteria

Average deep sleep duration increases by 15+ minutes during the experiment period compared to the baseline period.

## Data requirements

Participants who opt in will contribute:

- [ ] Average deep sleep (baseline vs experiment)
- [ ] Average total sleep (baseline vs experiment)
- [ ] Average sleep efficiency (baseline vs experiment)
- [ ] Average morning energy rating (baseline vs experiment)
- [ ] Self-reported adherence percentage

**All data is anonymized.** Only aggregated averages are shared, no raw daily data.

## Risks & Limitations

- Seasonal light changes could affect sleep independently
- Participants may compensate with more morning caffeine
- Placebo effect — knowing you're in an experiment affects behavior
- Sleep trackers have varying accuracy for deep sleep measurement
- Individual caffeine metabolism varies significantly (CYP1A2 gene)
